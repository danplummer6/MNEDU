Frequently Asked Questions

How does the process work?

Most groups get an idea of how many students would be interested. Once there is enough interest to proceed you will need to collect the fees from each student and contract the charter.

What if rain is in the forecast?

Unless there is a major event like a hurricane the trip will operate as scheduled. Ponchos will be provided and you are welcome to bring umbrellas. Also some of the trips itinerary may be adjusted to accommodate for weather. This may include spending more time at the museum or going to indoor sights during forecasted inclement weather.

What’s included in the price?

The price covers airfare, motor coaches with Wi-Fi and bathrooms, bus drivers who are licensed tour guides, breakfast, lunch and dinner, custom T shirts for each participant, a tour coordinator, water on each bus and all applicable fees and taxes. Almost all sights are included in the $480 per person price, but a few select stops like the Ford Theater would require an additional $6 per person. Please note, the bus drivers are licensed tour guides and give narration while on the bus, but once the bus stops the group is on its own as the bus drivers need to stay with the bus.

What if one of the students can’t make the trip after already paying?

The price is based on 158 passengers paying $480 per person and 3 free teachers/chaperons for a total of 161 participants in your group. If your group has enough notice, another person can take their seat but last minute cancelations would not be refundable.

What if we only have 157 passengers interested?

As previously stated the $480 per person price is based on 158 paying passengers. If there are only 157 passengers each person would have to pay an additional $3.00 to cover the 1 missing passenger.

Why would we want to book a day trip to DC?

The average cost of a student 3-day trip is $2,000. With hotels, extra chaperones and additional meals the cost can be very unattainable for many groups. During our action packed day trips, the students will get to see all of the same historical monuments as a 3-day trip without the added expense or liability.

What sights will we see?

The stops are customizable by your group but here are some recommendations: White House (Photo Stop), U.S Capital Building (either guided tour, on your own inside, or photo stop), Washington Monument, World War II Memorial, Dr. Martin Luther King Jr Memorial, Lincoln Memorial, Vietnam Veterans Memorial, Arlington National Cemetery/Changing of the guards (for an additional $8.50 per person a guided tram tour can be provided at Arlington), Korean War Memorial, Jefferson Memorial, Iwo Jima Memorial, 9/11 Pentagon Memorial, Kennedy Center, Smithsonian Museums (traditional or air and space). If groups want to spend more time at the museum then some of the memorial stops can be cut out.

How are seats assigned?

Since you are chartering the aircraft the only people onboard will be with your group. No seat assignments will be given but first class and exit row seats are usually reserved for chaperones and teachers.

Will there be seats sold to others not in our group?

No, your charter will be exclusively for your group.

What if I would like a reference?

If you would like a reference please let us know and we will provide a contact for you.

What if we don't have enough students at our school to fill all 159 seats?

It is ok to partner with another school, for example some private schools are smaller in size but can team up with another school with similar circumstances.
